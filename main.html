I'm currently a Master's student in geology at Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris aiming to pursue for a PhD focused on geological and hydrogeological processes related to geothermal systems.

Trained in naturalist methods at the University of Toulouse and in quantitative approaches at IPGP, I aim to bridge these two perspectives to build rigorous, data-driven models of geological systems, primarily implemented in Python.

02 — Career project

My
interest

01 🔥
Geothermy

I have a keen of interest for geothermal exploration and research. The scientific questions raised by geothermal systems from structural geology and geophysical exploration to reservoir modeling are what drive me most, but I am equally motivated by the sustainable potential of geothermal energy and its societal dimension, particularly for the electrification of non-interconnected zones.

02 🌋
Geodynamic & Tectonic

I really like tectonic, from structures charactarization to geomorphologic observation. This area of geology integrated really large scales of time and space, allowing us to learn more and more from the earth dynamic. Understanding deformation and its impact on water flows is the key to geothermal and karst modelling.

03 ⚙️
Numerical modeling

Through my academic training, I developed an interest for coding and numerical modeling that bridges field observation and quantitative analysis.
